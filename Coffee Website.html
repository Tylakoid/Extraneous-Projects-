How to make an espresso?

An espresso is one of the most simplest coffees on the menu.
One shot of espresso should contain 30ml, but it can vary depending on whether a person wants a strong or weak coffee. When measuring the amount of coffee beans we should add, place you thumb on the centre of the machine and hold the lever and pull.
Once you have completed measuring the amount of coffee beans, make sure to press down the coffee beans with 18kg of weight, then proceed to carefully tighten it on to the coffee machine and press the button for 30mls of water.
The coffee should have a nice brown colour and CREMA!!!

Item Image Usage
Coffee Grinder Coffee Grinder are used to grind coffee beans into fine powder, which can be used to produce coffee. Grinded coffee beans only last for an hour, so it is not necessary to have a big hopper or grind a large amount.
Tamper Coffee tampers are used to compress the coffee, around 18kg of pressure should be apply, this insure the water does not flow through to quickly (creating light coloured coffee) or flow through too slowing (created black burnt coffee).
Coffee beans Depending on the coffee bean, may of the processes will slightly alter to suit the coffee bean. A good coffee relies on a good bean, however experience is needed to bring out the full flavour of the bean.
Coffee Machine Coffee Machines are used to make the coffee, by adding hot water to the grinded coffee beans which are filtered through a basket, it also has a steam wand which is used to froth milk.
Cleaning agents Cleaning agents are used to clean the coffee machine, which need a lot of care, and a clean almost everyday you use it.
Milk Jug with thermometer Milk jugs contain measurements, in order to measure the milk, however we can also do this by approximation. Along with the milk jug, is the thermometer, which will measure the temperature of the milk while you froth it.

Some tips to remember!

  • Turn on the coffee machine 15 minutes before making coffee.
  • Coffee beans in the hopper (that have been grinded) only last for an hour.
  • Always remember to regularly clean and pruge the steam wand, do not cross contaminate!
